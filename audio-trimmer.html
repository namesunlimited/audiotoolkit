Free Online Audio Trimmer & Cutter

Whether you're pulling a favorite 30 seconds out of a longer recording, removing dead air from the start of a voice memo, or cutting a clip down to fit a specific length, trimming audio is one of the most common small edits people need to make. This tool lets you do it visually — see the waveform, drag the start and end points exactly where you want them, and export just that section.

There's nothing to install and nothing to upload. The entire process — reading your file, drawing the waveform, cutting the selection, and exporting the result — happens using your browser's own audio engine.

How to Trim an Audio File

  • Upload your file. Click the upload area or drag your audio file in. The waveform will appear automatically once it's decoded.
  • Drag the handles. Two handles appear on the waveform — one for the start point, one for the end point. Drag either one to adjust your selection.
  • Preview it. Use "Play Selection" to hear exactly what you're about to export, so you can fine-tune the cut before committing to it.
  • Choose a format and download. Pick WAV for lossless quality or MP3 for a smaller file, then hit "Trim & Download."

Why Trim Visually Instead of by Typing Timestamps?

Typing in exact start and end times sounds precise, but it's slow — you usually end up guessing, listening back, and adjusting the numbers several times before it's right. Seeing the actual waveform makes this much faster: quiet sections show up as flat, low-amplitude stretches, while speech or music shows up as visibly denser waves. That means you can often spot exactly where a sentence starts or a song's intro ends just by looking, without needing to listen through the whole file first.

Common Reasons to Trim Audio

  • Cutting a highlight or quote out of a longer podcast episode or interview
  • Removing silence or throat-clearing from the start or end of a voice recording
  • Creating a short clip from a song for a video, presentation, or social media post
  • Shortening a voice memo down to just the relevant part before sharing it
  • Preparing a sound effect or notification tone from a longer audio source

WAV vs. MP3 for Your Trimmed Clip

If your trimmed clip is headed into further editing — say, into a video project or a Digital Audio Workstation — export as WAV to keep it lossless and avoid stacking compression on top of compression. If the clip is in its final form and you just need something easy to share or upload, MP3 keeps the file size small without a noticeable quality difference for most listening situations.

Tips for a Clean Cut

  • Zoom your attention on the waveform near your cut points — a cut placed mid-word or mid-note is far more noticeable than one placed in a natural pause
  • Always preview your selection with "Play Selection" before exporting, especially for spoken-word content where an abrupt cut can sound jarring
  • If you need multiple clips from the same file, trim and download one at a time, then re-upload the original for the next cut
Is this audio trimmer safe and private? ▾
Yes. Your audio file is processed entirely inside your browser and is never uploaded to a server.
Can I preview my trimmed audio before downloading? ▾
Yes, use the Play Selection button to hear exactly what will be exported before you download it.
Will trimming reduce the audio quality? ▾
No. Trimming only removes the parts outside your selection — it doesn't re-compress or alter the sound of the part you keep, unless you choose an MP3 export at a lower bitrate.
How precise is the trimming? ▾
You can drag the handles on the waveform for quick adjustments, letting you get a precise cut visually without typing timestamps.
What file formats can I export? ▾
You can export your trimmed clip as WAV (lossless) or MP3 (compressed, smaller file size).
