Free Online Audio to Text

Typing everything out by hand isn't always the fastest way to get words down — sometimes speaking is quicker, especially for drafting notes, brainstorming, or dictating a quick message. This tool uses your browser's built-in speech recognition to turn your spoken words into text live, as you talk, so you can capture ideas without touching the keyboard.

Click the microphone button, allow microphone access when prompted, and start speaking. Your words will appear in the text box as the browser recognizes them.

Live Dictation vs. File Transcription

It's worth being upfront about what this tool does and doesn't do: it transcribes speech as you speak it, in real time, through your microphone. Browsers don't currently provide a way for a webpage to feed a pre-recorded audio file into their speech recognition engine — that functionality is designed specifically around live microphone input.

If you need to transcribe an existing recording, one workaround is to play the recording aloud near your microphone while this tool listens — results will vary in accuracy depending on your speakers, microphone, and background noise, but it can work in a pinch for shorter clips.

A Note on Privacy

Unlike the other tools on this site, this one isn't fully private by default. Speech recognition in Chrome and Edge relies on a cloud-based service to convert your voice into text — meaning your spoken audio is sent to an external server for processing, not analyzed entirely on your device. This is a limitation of the browser's built-in speech API itself, not something controlled by this website. If privacy is a strict requirement, keep this in mind before dictating sensitive information.

Tips for Better Accuracy

  • Speak clearly and at a natural, steady pace — very fast or very quiet speech reduces accuracy
  • Use a quiet environment; background noise is one of the biggest causes of misrecognized words
  • Select the correct language and accent variant from the dropdown before you start
  • Pause briefly between sentences to give the recognition engine a clear boundary to work with

Common Uses

  • Quickly drafting notes, emails, or messages by speaking instead of typing
  • Brainstorming out loud and capturing ideas as text without breaking your flow to type
  • Accessibility support for people who find speaking easier or faster than typing
  • Quick voice memos that you want in text form right away
Can I upload an audio file to transcribe it? ▾
No. This tool transcribes live speech from your microphone as you talk. Browsers don't provide a way to feed a pre-recorded audio file into their speech recognition engine.
Is my voice sent to a server? ▾
This depends on your browser. Chrome and Edge use a cloud-based speech recognition service to convert your voice to text, meaning audio is processed on external servers, not entirely on your device. This is a browser-level feature, not something this website controls.
Which browsers support this tool? ▾
Speech recognition works best in Chrome and Edge. Safari and Firefox have limited or no support for the underlying Web Speech API.
Can I download the transcribed text? ▾
Yes, once you've finished dictating, you can copy the text or download it as a plain text (.txt) file.
