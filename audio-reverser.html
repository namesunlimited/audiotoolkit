Free Online Audio Reverser

Playing a sound backward reveals a completely different texture — cymbals swell instead of decay, spoken words become an unrecognizable string of syllables, and simple melodies can take on an eerie or unexpected character. Reversing audio is a classic sound design trick used in music production, video editing, and creative audio experiments of all kinds.

This tool reverses your audio file entirely inside your browser. It reads the audio data, flips the order of every sample from end to start, and lets you preview and download the result — nothing is uploaded to a server at any point.

How Audio Reversal Works

An audio file is really just a very long sequence of numbers, each representing the sound wave's position at a tiny slice of time. Reversing the audio means reading that sequence backward — the last sample becomes the first, and the first becomes the last — so the sound plays in the opposite direction from how it was originally recorded. No audio data is removed or altered in the process, only reordered.

Common Uses for Reversed Audio

  • Music production: reversed cymbal or piano swells are a common transition effect leading into a new section of a track
  • Sound design: reversing a recorded sound effect often produces an interesting new texture that wouldn't be achievable any other way
  • Creative experiments: some musicians and podcasters use reversed clips as intentional novelty or transition moments
  • Voice and audio analysis: reversing speech is sometimes used in linguistics or audio research to study patterns independent of forward meaning

Tips for Working with Reversed Audio

  • Always preview the reversed clip before downloading — reversed audio can sound very different from what you'd expect just from imagining it
  • If you're using a reversed clip as a transition effect, try trimming it first so only the interesting part of the reversed sound is included
  • Combining a reversed clip with a fade-in often smooths out an otherwise abrupt-sounding reversed swell
