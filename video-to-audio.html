Free Online Video to Audio Extractor

Sometimes it's the sound, not the picture, that you actually need — a song from a music video, a voiceover from a recorded presentation, an interview clip, or a sample from a longer video project. Re-recording it from scratch is unnecessary when the audio is already embedded right there in the video file. This tool pulls it out directly and saves it as a standalone MP3 or WAV file.

As with every tool here, the entire process runs locally in your browser. The video file is read from your device, its audio track is decoded and extracted, and the result is handed back to you as a download — nothing is uploaded to a server.

How Audio Extraction Works

Video files are actually containers holding two separate streams: a video track and an audio track, bundled together. Extracting audio simply means reading the video file, decoding its audio stream using your browser's built-in audio engine, and re-encoding that stream on its own as MP3 or WAV — the video portion is discarded entirely, leaving just the sound.

Choosing WAV or MP3 for Extracted Audio

If you plan to edit the extracted audio further — cleaning it up, adjusting levels, or mixing it with other tracks — export as WAV to preserve full quality through your editing process. If you just need the audio in its final, shareable form (a podcast clip, a sound bite, a voice memo pulled from a video), MP3 keeps the file small without a major quality trade-off.

Common Reasons to Extract Audio from Video

  • Pulling a voiceover or narration track out of a recorded presentation or screen capture
  • Creating a podcast-style audio version of a video interview
  • Saving background music or a sound effect that only exists inside a video file
  • Extracting a lecture or meeting recording's audio for easier note-taking or transcription
  • Getting just the audio from a video you recorded yourself, to repurpose elsewhere

Tips for Best Results

  • Use a video with clear, high-quality audio to begin with — extraction can't improve on audio that was recorded poorly in the first place
  • If the extracted audio sounds quiet, run it through a volume booster or normalizer afterward
  • For long videos, extraction may take a little longer since the whole audio track needs to be decoded before it can be exported
What video formats can I upload? ▾
Most common formats your browser can play, including MP4, WebM, and MOV, work with this tool.
Is the video itself uploaded to a server? ▾
No. The entire process, from reading the video file to extracting and encoding its audio track, happens locally in your browser.
Will the extracted audio be the same quality as in the video? ▾
Choosing WAV keeps the extracted audio at full quality. Choosing MP3 uses standard lossy compression, with quality depending on the bitrate you select.
Can I extract audio from a large video file? ▾
Yes, though very large video files may take longer to process, since decoding happens using your own device's processing power rather than a remote server.
