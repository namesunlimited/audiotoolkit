Free Online Noise Reducer

A constant hiss, fan hum, or low-level background rumble is one of the most common problems in home-recorded audio — voice memos, podcast recordings, or anything captured without professional soundproofing. This tool reduces that steady background noise using two techniques working together: a filter that removes very low-frequency rumble, and a noise gate that quiets the signal during gaps and quiet passages where the unwanted noise is most noticeable.

All processing happens locally in your browser. Your file is decoded, cleaned up, and handed back as a download — nothing is uploaded to a server.

How This Noise Reducer Works

High-Pass Filtering

Many common noise sources — air conditioning hum, electrical buzz, handling rumble from a microphone stand — sit in the very low end of the frequency range, well below where human speech and most music actually live. A high-pass filter removes that low-frequency energy while leaving speech and music untouched, cutting down on rumble immediately.

Noise Gating

A noise gate listens to the overall level of the audio and automatically quiets it further whenever it drops below a chosen threshold — typically during pauses between words, gaps in music, or any section that should be near-silent. This is where background hiss is usually most noticeable, so gating it down makes the recording sound noticeably cleaner during those moments, without affecting the louder parts where your voice or music is actually playing.

Choosing a Reduction Strength

  • Light — a subtle cleanup, best for recordings that are already fairly clean but have a slight background hum
  • Medium — a balanced setting that noticeably reduces common background noise while keeping voice and music sounding natural
  • Strong — the most aggressive setting, best for noticeably noisy recordings, though it can occasionally soften very quiet details along with the noise

If you're not sure where to start, Medium is a safe default for most voice recordings and podcasts.

What This Tool Can and Can't Fix

This approach works best on steady, constant background noise — the kind that's present throughout a recording at roughly the same level. It's less effective at removing noise that's tangled up with the wanted audio at the same volume and time, like a voice recorded very close to a loud fan running constantly in the background at speaking volume — that kind of overlapping noise requires more advanced, model-based audio processing to separate cleanly.

Tips for Best Results

  • Always preview before downloading — the right strength setting varies a lot depending on how noisy the original recording is
  • If your recording has both rumble and hiss, this tool addresses both at once, but very heavy noise may need multiple passes or professional audio software for full removal
  • Prevention is still the best fix — recording in a quieter environment or closer to the microphone reduces how much noise reduction you'll need afterward
