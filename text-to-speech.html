Free Online Text to Speech

Whether you want to proofread your writing by ear, hear a message read back before sending it, or just prefer listening over reading, converting text to speech is a simple way to change how you take in written content. This tool uses your browser's own built-in speech engine — the same technology behind screen readers and voice assistants — to read any text you type or paste, instantly.

Because it relies on your browser and operating system's speech engine rather than a remote service, there's nothing to upload and no waiting: press play, and it reads immediately.

Why There's No Download Option

Unlike the other tools on this site, text-to-speech works a little differently under the hood. Browsers generate the spoken audio live, through your device's speakers, using an API designed purely for real-time playback — not for producing a file you can save. There's no standard, privacy-respecting way for a website to capture and export that audio as an MP3 or WAV without routing it through a server, which would defeat the "everything stays on your device" approach this tool is built around.

If you specifically need a downloadable voice-over file, that typically requires a dedicated server-based text-to-speech service — this tool is best suited for quick listening, not file generation.

Adjusting Voice, Speed, and Pitch

  • Voice — pick from whichever voices are installed on your device. Options and quality vary by operating system and browser.
  • Speed — slow it down to catch every word clearly, or speed it up to get through longer text faster.
  • Pitch — raise or lower the tone of the voice. Most people find the default setting sounds most natural.

Common Uses for Text to Speech

  • Proofreading your own writing by listening for awkward phrasing that's easy to miss when reading silently
  • Having an article, email, or document read aloud while doing something else
  • Previewing how a message or script will sound when spoken before recording or sending it
  • Making written content more accessible for people who prefer or need audio over text

Tips for Best Results

  • Break very long text into shorter paragraphs — some browsers handle shorter chunks more reliably than one enormous block of text
  • Try a few different installed voices — quality and naturalness can vary noticeably between them
  • If a sentence sounds off, check for unusual punctuation or abbreviations, which speech engines sometimes read literally
Can I download the generated speech as an audio file? ▾
No. This tool uses your browser's built-in speech engine to play text aloud instantly, but browsers don't provide a way to export that audio as a downloadable file without a server-based service.
Which voices are available? ▾
The available voices depend on your device and browser. Most computers and phones include several built-in voices in different languages and accents.
Is my text sent to a server? ▾
No. Speech is generated locally using your browser's or operating system's built-in text-to-speech engine. Your text is never uploaded anywhere.
Why do the available voices look different on my phone vs my computer? ▾
Voices are provided by your device's operating system and browser, not by this website, so the exact list varies between Windows, macOS, Android, and iOS.
