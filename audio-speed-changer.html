Free Online Audio Speed Changer

Sometimes you want to get through a long recording faster — a lecture, a podcast, a meeting you're catching up on. Other times you want the opposite: slowing something down to catch every word, learn a musical passage, or transcribe a fast talker accurately. This tool handles both directions, from half-speed up to double-speed, and can keep the pitch natural even at the extremes so a sped-up voice doesn't sound like a chipmunk.

Everything happens locally in your browser — your audio file is read from your device, processed, and handed back as a download without ever being uploaded anywhere.

Preserve Pitch vs. Natural Pitch Shift

There are two different ways to change playback speed, and they sound quite different:

Preserve Pitch (recommended for most uses)

This mode changes how fast the audio plays without changing how high or low it sounds — a sped-up voice still sounds like a normal voice, just talking faster. It works by reconstructing the audio in small overlapping segments and reassembling them at the new speed, which keeps pitch natural but can introduce very slight artifacts, especially at more extreme speed settings.

Natural Pitch Shift (Preserve Pitch turned off)

This mode works exactly like speeding up or slowing down a tape or record — pitch rises when you speed up, and drops when you slow down. It's simpler and introduces no processing artifacts, but a voice sped up this way will sound noticeably higher-pitched, which isn't always desirable.

Common Uses for Changing Audio Speed

  • Faster listening: Getting through long podcasts, lectures, or meeting recordings at 1.25x-1.5x speed without losing comprehension
  • Transcription: Slowing a recording down to 0.75x or 0.5x to catch every word accurately while typing it out
  • Language learning: Slowing down native-speed audio to practice listening comprehension at a manageable pace
  • Music practice: Slowing down a song to learn a fast passage note-by-note, without shifting it out of its original key
  • Content editing: Fitting a voiceover or clip into a specific time slot by nudging its speed slightly

Choosing the Right Speed

For most spoken-word content, 1.25x to 1.5x speed is the sweet spot for faster listening without losing clarity — most people adjust to this range within a few minutes. Going beyond 1.75x usually starts to feel rushed and can reduce comprehension. For slowing things down, 0.75x is a gentle reduction useful for tricky sections, while 0.5x is better suited to detailed transcription or note-for-note music practice, where you need maximum clarity over natural pacing.

Tips for Best Results

  • Keep "Preserve Pitch" on for spoken-word content — an unnaturally high or low voice is harder to understand, which defeats the purpose of the adjustment
  • For music practice, try turning "Preserve Pitch" on so you can slow a song down without it sounding out of tune with the sheet music or your instrument
  • Preview your selected speed before committing — moderate settings (0.75x-1.5x) tend to sound cleanest with pitch preservation
  • If the pitch-preserved result sounds slightly off at extreme speeds, try a less aggressive setting closer to 1x
Can I change speed without changing the pitch? ▾
Yes. Turn on 'Preserve Pitch' and the tool will keep the pitch the same while still speeding up or slowing down the playback.
What speed range can I use? ▾
You can slow audio down to half speed (0.5x) or speed it up to double speed (2x), with fine control in between.
Why does pitch-preserved audio sometimes sound slightly different? ▾
Preserving pitch while changing speed requires reconstructing the audio in small overlapping segments, which can introduce minor artifacts, especially at extreme speed settings. Moderate speed changes (0.75x-1.5x) usually sound cleanest.
Is my audio file uploaded anywhere? ▾
No. All processing happens locally in your browser. Your file is never uploaded to a server.
